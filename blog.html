COCHIN

Good pets, the Cochin do well in a backyard. They seldom cackle even when laying. The hens make great mothers.

SILKY

Silkies are a friendly breed who are very easy to train to eat out of your hand. Silkie hens are one of the most famous brooders, making them a great choice as foster mothers.

POLISH BREED

The Polish breed is best known for their magnificent V-shaped crest, making them great as show birds. They also have a decent egg laying ability, though this isn’t as reliable as other breeds.

BROILER

bred and raised specifically for meat production.

PLYMOUTH ROCK

the most popular breed in the US due to their excellent egg laying and hearty frames that make for a good meal. They’re also easy to tame and non-aggressive. What’s not to like?

JAPANESE BANTAM

The Japanese Bantam is a breed that only comes in the one size: Small. They serve as great pets and show birds.

BRAHMA

The Brahma can do everything, from lay eggs to become a filling dinner to win awards at a breed show.

RHODE ISLAND RED

They are very low maintenance, and can tolerate less than favorable conditions. Hens lay about 5 – 7 eggs per week.

ASEEL

The Aseel is a very strong and aggresive breed, known to fight at all times. However, they are very docile toward humans. They serve as a good foundation stock for meat producers.

NAKED NECK

The Naked Neck breed has no feathers around the neck, hence the name. However, they’re excellent dual purpose birds and are very resilient to most diseases, making them a great choice for beginners.

SEBRIGHT

The Sebright is a bantam built for the show world. They are very active birds, but they aren’t aggressive and aren’t very loud, so this is a great chicken for someone needing an easy time.

FAVORELLES

Favorelles are extremely docile and quiet, A well cared for hen will lay about 4 pinkish colored eggs per week.
